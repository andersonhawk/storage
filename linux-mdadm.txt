
## Linux RAID
## Implemented through MD driver, managed with mdadm

	$ mdadm --examine /dev/sdd /dev/sde
## partition sdb and sdc, and choose raid partition type (fd), use
## mdadmin examine the partition again.
	$ mdadm --examine /dev/sdd1 /dev/sde1 (no md superblock detected)

## create mirror level raid
	$ mdadm --create /dev/md0 --level=mirror --raid-devices=2 /dev/sdd1 /dev/sde1

## cat /proc/mdstat to check raid device building process, use mdadm --examine
## to check device info again.

	$ mdadm --detail /dev/md0

## add a spare member to a mirror raid device
	$ mdadm --manage /dev/md0 --add /dev/sdf1
## convert spare device to raid device in a mirror raid device
	$ mdadm --grow --raid-devices=3 /dev/md0

## fail one of member in raid device
	$ mdadm --fail /dev/md0 /dev/sdb1
	$ mdadm --grow --raid-devices=2 /dev/md0
	$ mdadm --detail /dev/md0

## add sdb1 to a spare member in raid device
	$ mdadm --zero-superblock /dev/sdb1
	$ mdadm --manage /dev/md0 --add /dev/sdb1


## hotplug one device in raid device (status degraded, auto rebuild)
	$ mdadm --detail /dev/md0


## config file (/etc/mdadm.conf)
	$ mdadm --detail --scan --verbose >> /etc/mdadm/mdadm.conf
